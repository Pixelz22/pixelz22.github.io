A text-based adventure game about escaping from a Prisoner-of-War camp in Cold War Era Russia.

It's been four years since the storm... four years since you crashed that plane on Russian soil. Since that unforgettable day, your life has been a matter of survival, whether from the harsh treatment of the guards, or the taunts and side-eyed looks of other prisoners. You've were just about to resign yourself to your fate, when a faint glimmer of hope appears one fateful morning. You're getting out of here. You have to.

Play as American pilot Corinne Dumont as she attempts to sneak her way out of a Russian prisoner-of-war camp. Talk to guards and fellow prisoners. Collect information, and use it to manipulate the setting to your advantage. Everything runs on a schedule here: patience will be your ally.

This was made as a project for my CSC 281 (Game Design) class at NC State University. The whole game was made using the Twine engine.

Remarks

  • My first ever narrative-focused game!
  • The game uses HTML hyperlinks to move around the world and advance the story.
  • There were originally plans for 3 other storylines and endings, but they had to be cut due to time constraints.
